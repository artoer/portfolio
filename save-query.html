Search Alert Feature

Research, flow and design of a search alert functionality keeping users poised for deals

For my Internship at Talpa eCommerce as a UX designer in Summer 2022

Overview

VakantieVeilingen is a platform owned by Talpa eCommerce that specializes in selling a variety of products by auctioning them among users.

During my internship I was tasked to conceptualize a feature that would enable users to save, and be notified of, auctions that match their interests by allowing users to save their search keywords.

My Role

  • Competitive analysis
  • User flows
  • Illustration
  • Iterative mobile-first design
  • Stakeholder management

Tools Used

  • Sketch
  • Miro
  • Jira

Process

Competitive Analysis and User Flows

My approach began with a thorough study of the existing user flow and a competitive analysis to identify similar features on other platforms.

Using Miro, I created a user flow to visualize the proposed feature's functionality and design elements. Doing this helped me inventorize the screens and iteractions my designs had to account for.

Iterative Design

After, I used Sketch to iteratively design the feature screens, icons, and email notifications. I presented my designs to the team, incorporating their feedback into the iterative design process.

Handover

Returning to Miro, I integrated the finalized screens into the user flows, communicating the proposed feature's user experience and functionality for further implementation by sharing it in the Jira ticket, providing the development team with guidance for the next phase of implementation.

Solution

My solution enables users to set alerts for new search results directly from the search page or their account. Users can easily manage these alerts, including editing and deleting them, and view matching results. Additionally, I propose an email notification design with the option to disable alerts directly from the inbox.

Potential Impact

The new search alert functionality enhances user engagement by helping them discover specific auctions, even when unavailable, ultimately providing a follow-up step for potential conversions.

Final Thoughts

This was a smaller task during my internship that taught me a lot about the importance of user flows, in this case the user could interact with the feature in many different ways, resulting in many states that had to be accounted for. Mapping these interaction flows gave me a clear idea of what screens needed to be crafted before even opening Sketch. I also found the completed user flow, supplemented by my designs, especially useful for the collection of feedback and the final handover.

Unfortunately, there was no opportunity during my internship to test or gather user feedback on the feature. However, if I had the chance now, I would probably start, at the very least, with a painted door test to gauge user interest in the feature and viability before committing resources on development.
