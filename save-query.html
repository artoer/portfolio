Talpa eCommerce

Designing a features that saves and notifies users of new search results for VakantieVeilingen, an auction style e-commerce website.

Overview

For my internship at Talpa eCommerce, I was tasked with designing a feature for their e-commerce website VakantieVeilingen, a concept that enables users to get good deals on concerts, holidays and miscellaneous products by bidding on them in an online auction.

The proposed feature would allow users to save their search queries and receive alerts when new products that match their search become available. This feature is expected to provide users with relevant products via email alerts, while also providing insights into user demand.

Research

I started by studying the current user flow and conducting a brief competitive analysis to identify similar features on other websites, such as job boards and marketplaces. Using Miro, I created a flowchart to map out how the feature would work for our platform and identified the necessary design elements.

Design

With the UX flow mapped out, I proceeded to design the screens for the feature. I wanted to visualize how the toggle switch would be integrated into the search page and how the alert page would look for users. I took screenshots of the existing pages and imported them into Sketch, where I added the new design elements on top of them. I then presented my findings and designs to the team, which provided me with valuable feedback on my progress.

I also created several icons to be used within the designs, as well as visualized how the email notifications associated with the feature could look. The email notifications are an integral part of the flow, so it was important to consider their design. I created the icons within Sketch using tools such as the line and pen tools. For the email design, I reviewed other email templates from the same brand that are currently live, such as newsletters, and incorporated the header and footer for consistency.

Challenges

Designing the feature in a way that is compact and doesn't take up a lot of important screen real estate, while still being noticeable and easy to use, was a significant challenge. I had to consider how to balance the space needed for the new feature with the need to keep important, KPI-related content such as the auction listings visible and accessible.

Another challenge was designing for the different ways users can add, edit, or delete search query alerts, including within their profile, on the search page, or through email notifications. To address this, I spent time considering the various user scenarios and made sure the feature was easily accessible from multiple locations.

Unfortunately, we didn't have the opportunity to gather user feedback or metrics on the feature during my internship. However, if I were to test it now, one method I would consider is the painted door test. This involves creating a mockup or prototype and testing it with a small group of users to gather feedback on its usability and effectiveness. By doing so, we could test its viability before committing significant development resources to the feature.

Handoff

After completing the design for all screens, I finalized the design process by incorporating feedback from the internship supervisor into the Sketch designs and checking with a UX designer colleague to ensure that the designs align with the brand guidelines. Fortunately, he found the designs to be visually appealing, with only minor tweaks needed. The designs are now ready for handoff to the development team for implementation.

Conclusion

Working on the feature was a valuable experience that allowed me to practice my skills in research, interaction design, illustration and collaboration with team members. Overall, I am proud of the final designs and confident that they will enhance the user experience on our platform by providing users with a convenient way to save their search queries and receive relevant product alerts. While I do not expect my design to be implemented verbatim in the future, it serves as a solid foundation for further development.
