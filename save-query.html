Talpa eCommerce

Saved Search Notification for VakantieVeilingen

Overview

About the Project

This project, undertaken during my internship at Talpa eCommerce, involved designing a feature for their e-commerce website, VakantieVeilingen. The objective was to enable users to save their search queries and receive alerts when new products matching their search criteria became available.

Context

This was a real-world project as part of my internship, and my responsibilities included mapping out the UX flow, designing screens, creating icons, and developing email notifications for the proposed feature.

Challenges and Constraints

The main challenge was to create a compact feature that didn't dominate screen real estate while remaining noticeable and user-friendly. Additionally, I had to consider various methods users might employ to add, edit, or delete search query alerts, including within their profiles, on the search page, or through email notifications.

Role and Team

As a UX designer, I led the UX flow mapping, screen design, and the creation of associated icons and email notifications. Collaboration within the team was vital to refining the designs based on feedback.

Deliverables

I provided Sketch designs and a user flow ready for handoff to the development team for implementation.

Strategy

My approach began with a thorough study of the existing user flow and a competitive analysis to identify similar features on other platforms. Using Miro, I created a flowchart to visualize the proposed feature's functionality and design elements.

Process

Once the UX flow was established, I used Sketch to design the feature screens, icons, and email notifications. I presented my designs to the team, incorporating their feedback into the iterative design process.

Result

Upon completing the design phase, I incorporated feedback from my internship supervisor and a UX designer colleague to ensure alignment with brand guidelines. The designs ready for handoff to the development team for implementation.

Validation

Unfortunately, there was no opportunity during my internship to test or gather user feedback on the feature. However, if I had the chance now, I would probably start, at the very least, with a painted door test to gauge user interest in the feature and viability before committing resources on development.
