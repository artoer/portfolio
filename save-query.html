Talpa eCommerce

Saved Search Notification for VakantieVeilingen

Overview

About the project

The task for my internship at Talpa eCommerce was to design a feature for their e-commerce website, VakantieVeilingen. The feature would allow users to save their search queries and receive alerts when new products that match their search become available.

My role

As a UX designer, I mapped out the UX flow, designed the screens, created icons, and email notifications associated with the feature.

Challenges

The challenge was to design the feature in a way that is compact and doesn't take up a lot of important screen real estate while still being noticeable and easy to use. I also had to consider designing for the different ways users can add, edit, or delete search query alerts, including within their profile, on the search page, or through email notifications.

Deliverables

Sketch designs and user flow ready for handoff to the development team for implementation.

Strategy

I started by studying the current user flow and conducting a competitive analysis to identify similar features on other websites. I created a flowchart using Miro to map out how the feature would work for our platform and identified the necessary design elements.

Process

With the UX flow mapped out, I designed the screens for the feature in Sketch. I also created icons and visualized how the email notifications associated with the feature could look. I presented my designs to the team and incorporated their feedback into the Sketch designs.

Final Designs

Result

After completing the design for all screens, I finalized the design process by incorporating feedback from my internship supervisor and checking with a UX designer colleague to ensure that the designs align with the brand guidelines. The designs are now ready for handoff to the development team for implementation.

Unfortunately, we didn't have the opportunity to gather user feedback or metrics on the feature during my internship. However one potential method I would employ now to test the viability of the feature is the painted door test before committing significant development resources to the feature.
