Talpa eCommerce

Designing a mobile-first quantity selector for Actie van de Dag, a deal-focused e-commerce website.

Overview

About the project

As an intern at Talpa eCommerce, I was assigned to design a solution for the e-commerce website Actie van de Dag, where customers could select the quantity of products they wanted to purchase on the product detail page, in addition to the shopping cart dropdown. The challenge was to make it work smoothly for mobile devices, with limited space in the sticky bottom bar.

My role

As the designer for this project, I took inventory of the current situation, gathered inspiration from competitors, designed thoughtfully and iteratively using Sketch, and presented various design options to my team, refining the final design based on feedback.

Challenges

The main challenge for this project was designing a solution that would work seamlessly for mobile users, given the limited space in the sticky bottom bar, while ensuring that all product options were easily accessible.

Deliverables

Handover ready Sketch designs of the functionality that consider the various scenarios.

Strategy

I began by taking inventory of the current situation, discovering several factors that complicate simply adding a quantity selector to the sticky bottom bar on mobile, the main one being products that require some kind of choice, such as the color of a curtain or the day of a concert ticket.

I also researched which design patterns other e-commerce websites employ, and finally my I conducted a lot of experimental and iterative design, trying out many different design patterns to make the most of the real estate on the page.

Iterations

From here, it was an iterative design process in collaboration with my team where many different solutions were sketched.

Result

For mobile, the final design solution would be to have the price and call-to-action prominently displayed at the top of the page, allowing ample space for the button, price, quantity selector, and product options. The bottom menu bar would then serve as an anchor link to scroll the user towards any options the user would have to select and the price.
