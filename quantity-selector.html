Talpa eCommerce

Mobile Quantity Selector for Actie van de Dag

Overview

About the project

As an intern at Talpa eCommerce, I was assigned to design a solution for the e-commerce website Actie van de Dag, where customers could select the quantity of products they wanted to purchase on the product detail page, in addition to the shopping cart dropdown. This addition is important because certain products such as concert tickets are often bought in multiples.

My role

As the designer for this project, I took inventory of the current situation, gathered inspiration from competitors, designed thoughtfully and iteratively using Sketch, and presented various design options to my team, refining the final design based on feedback.

Challenges

The main challenge for this project was designing a solution that would incorporate a quantity selector into the existing design, which currently consists of a sticky bottom bar on mobile, which offered limited screen real estate for such an addition. Furthermore, I had to consider various scenarios, including worst-case ones where a product requires choosing several options.

Deliverables

Handover ready Sketch designs of the functionality that consider the various scenarios.

Strategy

I began by taking inventory of the current situation, discovering several factors that complicate simply adding a quantity selector to the sticky bottom bar on mobile, the main one being products that require some kind of choice, such as the color of a curtain or the day of a concert ticket.

I also researched which design patterns other e-commerce websites employ, and finally I conducted a lot of experimental and iterative design, trying out many different design patterns to make the most of the available screen real estate.

Iterations

From here, it was an iterative design process in collaboration with my team where many different solutions were sketched.

Result

For the final design, it was decided to display the options, price, and add-to-cart button at the top of the page, in addition to the sticky bottom bar. The sticky bottom bar only appears as the user scrolls below the fold, and it now includes a quantity selector and an add-to-cart button. If the user needs to choose one or more options, the button will scroll them to the top of the page.
