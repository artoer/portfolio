Refining the Product Page

How might we enhance the product details page for Actie van de Dag on mobile to reduce user friction and improve conversion?

Overview

I refined the product page of Actie van de Dag as part of my internship. I was tasked to explore ways to integrate a quantity selector on the page, which uses a sticky bottom bar on mobile.

Problem

Tweakers users love building PCs, but finding the best-value components can be tricky.

What if we could help them avoid mistakes and ensure they get the most bang for their buck?

Process

Getting to know the user

To understand how Tweakers users build PCs, I reached out and interviewed five users while also inviting thousands of Tweakers visitors to complete a survey.

Interviews

Even experienced PC builders make mistakes. Each user had made a mistake at some point building their PC, and the frustrating consequences thereof, such as lengthy return processes.

Surveys

I also learned a lot about what users think about when choosing components, price and performance were two key words came up a lot in the interviews, and the survey confirmed that a staggering 64.7% of Tweakers users prioritize price-performance over all else.

Analyzing the competition

In addition to user research, I analyzed other tools, I saw a great amount of inspiration, but also areas where our tool could offer a better user experience.

PCPartPicker

PCPartPicker does a great job giving feedback on user error. But the user experience was disjointed and lacked oversight, with the overview and different components spread out over multiple pages.

Newegg

On Newegg, the tool took the form of a drawer on the side, allowing the user to browse components while keeping track of their build‚Äîall in one screen.

Back to the drawing board, I sketched several ways to improve feed forward and feedback in the tool, as well as provide users a clear way to remedy potential errors. For the discoverability, I changed the tool to be expanded on default, instead of collapsed.

Reflection

Refining the Product Page

How might we enhance the product details page on mobile to reduce user friction and improve conversion?

Overview

Actie van de Dag is a platform owned by Talpa eCommerce that specializes in selling a variety of discounted products, from furniture and electronics to hotel stays.

User feedback gathered using Hotjar revealed several pain points on the product details page, particularly the inability to buy multiples of a product directly from this page, which was only possible in the shopping cart page.

My Role

  • Audit of the current state
  • Competitive analysis
  • Iterative mobile-first design
  • Stakeholder collaboration
  • A/B testing

Tools used

  • Hotjar
  • Sketch
  • Miro
  • Jira
  • Sitespect
  • HTML, CSS, and JavaScript

Process

Discovery

My approach began with an assessment of the existing situation, uncovering the constraints and complexities in adding a quantity selector to the sticky bottom bar.

Next, I explored design patterns employed by other e-commerce platforms before diving into Sketch.

Iterations

The iterative design process involved collaboration with my team, exploring various solutions through sketches and prototypes.

Solution

After iterative rounds and stakeholder feedback with the Product Owner, UX Designer, and CRO Specialist, we reached a collective decision. We acknowledged the limitations of the sticky bottom bar and opted to prioritize the call-to-action and quantity selector within it. Simultaneously, we recognized the potential above the fold to accommodate more complex interactions and information, while prominently displaying the call-to-action once more.

Designs

My designs mainly focused on three common scenarios and showcase the situation both above and below the fold.

The final designs were shared in a Jira ticket, ensuring that they were easily accessible for further consideration and implementation by the development team.

Potential Impact

It's hypothesized that allowing users to purchase multiples of products earlier in the journey reduces friction, resulting in increased conversions. This is particularly beneficial for products often sold in multiples, such as concert tickets.

Outcome

Follow-up Validation

As a continuation, and partially validating the solution, an A/B test was conducted to assess featuring the add-to-cart call-to-action both above the fold and during scroll. Tracking call-to-action clicks revealed a notable increase, supporting the positive impact of this adjustment and suggesting the potential for integrating a quantity selector feature.

Implementation

Some time after my internship ended, my design was able to lay the foundation for the current iteration of the product detail page.

Final Thoughts

This experience taught me the importance of trying different approaches before finding the right solution. Collaborating with peers and stakeholders provided valuable feedback. Additionally, working within the constraints of mobile-first design emphasized the need to consider alternative solutions and recognize limitations.
