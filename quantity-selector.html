Quantity Selector Design for Actie van de Dag

Problem

Actie van de Dag is a platform owned by Talpa eCommerce that specializes in selling a variety of discounted products, from furniture and electronics to hotel stays.

Insights from Hotjar showed that users were missing the ability to buy multiples of a product on the product detail page, as right now it was only possible in the shopping cart page.

My Role

    During my internship at Talpa eCommerce, I undertook the challenge of incorporating a quantity selector into the product detail page. My responsibilities included:

  • Audit of the current state
  • Competitive analysis
  • Iterative mobile-first design
  • Stakeholder collaboration

Tools used

  • Hotjar
  • Sketch
  • Miro
  • Jira

Challenges and Constraints

  • Limited Screen Real Estate: Designing the "sticky bottom bar" for mobile, which already contains price and call-to-action.
  • Products with Variants: Managing additional screen space required for products with multiple options.
  • Alignment with Brand Guidelines: Crafting a design that fits seamlessly with the current layout and brand identity.

Solution

I provided Sketch designs ready for handover, accounting for various scenarios and considerations in the implementation of the quantity selector.

The final design addressed the challenge by displaying options, price, and add-to-cart button at the top of the page, complementing the sticky bottom bar. The latter appears upon scrolling, featuring a quantity selector and add-to-cart button. If options need selection, the button smoothly scrolls users to the top of the page.

Potential impact

By allowing users to purchase multiples of products earlier in the journey, friction was reduced, resulting in increased conversions. This is particularly beneficial for products often sold in multiples, such as concert tickets.

Process

Discovery

My approach began with an assessment of the existing situation, uncovering the constraints and complexities in adding a quantity selector to the sticky bottom bar.

After, I focused on exploring design patterns employed by other e-commerce platforms before diving into Sketch.

Iterations

The iterative design process involved collaboration with my team, exploring various solutions through sketches and prototypes.

Handover

The final designs were shared in a Jira ticket, ensuring that they were easily accessible for further consideration and implementation by the development team.
