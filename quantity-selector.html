Talpa eCommerce

Mobile Quantity Selector for Actie van de Dag

Overview

About the Project

During my internship at Talpa eCommerce, I undertook the challenge of enhancing the e-commerce experience for Actie van de Dag by implementing a mobile quantity selector on the product detail page and in the shopping cart dropdown. This feature was crucial for products like concert tickets, often purchased in multiples.

My Role

As the designated designer for this project, my responsibilities included assessing the current state, gathering inspiration from competitors, iteratively designing using Sketch, and presenting multiple design options to the team. The final design was refined based on collaborative feedback.

Challenges

The primary challenge was integrating a quantity selector into the existing mobile design, characterized by a sticky bottom bar with limited screen space. Addressing scenarios involving products with multiple options further complicated the task.

Deliverables

I provided Sketch designs ready for handover, accounting for various scenarios and considerations in the implementation of the quantity selector.

Strategy

My approach began with an in-depth assessment of the existing situation, uncovering complexities in adding a quantity selector to the sticky bottom bar. Extensive research on design patterns employed by other e-commerce platforms and numerous experimental iterations were key elements of the strategy.

Iterations

The iterative design process involved collaboration with my team, exploring various solutions through sketches and prototypes.

Result

The final design addressed the challenge by displaying options, price, and add-to-cart button at the top of the page, complementing the sticky bottom bar. The latter appears upon scrolling, featuring a quantity selector and add-to-cart button. If options need selection, the button smoothly scrolls users to the top of the page.
