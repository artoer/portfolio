Tweakers

Responsive Redesign for Tweakers

Problem

During my internship at Tweakers, I had the opportunity to contribute to the redesign of the website according to the new design system spearheaded by my supervisor, who also served as the UX designer. This involved implementing a responsive grid of up to 4 columns, necessitating careful consideration and experimentation in presenting various elements.

My role

My specific focus was on revamping the review portal and the review listings. Aligning my designs with the newly developed design system and grid, I took into account user behavior, preferences, and ensured responsiveness for smaller screen sizes. Surveys and data analysis of page interactions were integral in guiding these informed design decisions.

Process

Research

To determine the priority content for the redesign, we conducted surveys to understand what users considered important in the current situation. Additionally, I collaborated with the product owner to analyze user interactions on the page, adopting a data-driven design approach.

Sketches & Prototypes

My design process started with lo-fi sketches, exploring and experimenting with the right composition within the grid.

Designing for a widescreen format of 4 columns, as well as smaller screens (3 columns), tablets (2 columns), and mobile (1 column wide) posed a significant challenge. Finding a design that looked balanced across all screen sizes was crucial.

Result

I filled my designs with real content, and the team was satisfied with the final result. Contributing to the redesign of Tweakers was a fulfilling experience, and I hope to see my work on the website in the future.

Reflection

This was a smaller task that demonstrated the importance of guiding and justifying design choices through research and data. Responsive design presented a good challenge, with numerous iterations and feedback sessions with the team leading to a satisfying final result.
