Tweakers

PC Builder Tool for Tweakers

Problem

Tweakers.net is a Dutch consumer electronics and technology-focused website well known for its community, editorial content, and the Pricewatch, a comprehensive price comparison tool and important driver of website referals.

The project stemmed from the premise that Tweakers users, known for their enthusiasm in building PCs, would find value in a tool designed to assist them.

My Role

    As part of my graduation project, I independently managed the end-to-end design process for a conceptual PC Builder tool. My responsibilities included:

  • User research
  • Stakeholder management
  • Requirement definition
  • Brainstorming
  • Wireframing
  • Prototyping
  • User validation

Tools Used

  • Figma
  • FigJam
  • Google Forms
  • Google Meet (Remote interviews and testing)
  • Microsoft Teams (Remote interviews and testing)
  • HTML, CSS, and JavaScript (Prototyping)

Challenges and Constraints

  • User Needs: Providing value to Tweakers users, an audience already experienced with computers.
  • Product Design: Defining the tool's appearance, positioning within the website, and user interaction.
  • Technical Viability: Ensuring the backend supports required functionality and availability of usable component data.

Process

Discovery

User interviews validated my assumption and shed led light on key user motivations and goals.

Following up with a survey helped me validate and expand on some of my earlier findings.

Translating Painpoints into Requirements

    Based on my findings the customer journey was mapped, revealing opportunities and painpoints that informed the must-have requirements:

  • Ensure component compatibility, addressing a common pain point identified during discovery
  • Provide key component information, such as price-performance, which users highly value
  • Offer user recommendations, recognizing that users often rely on authoritative guidance
  • Prioritize user-friendliness, emphasizing intuitive design and usability

Stakeholder Follow-up

After reflecting on the requirements, I made sure to interview key stakeholders such as Tweakers editorial writers, component testers, and back-end developers, providing additional perspectives, technical insight and inspiration as I began working on a solution.

Ideation and Prototyping

At first I employed sketching and brainstorming techniques like Crazy Eight to synthesize rough but tangible ideas to meet product requirements.

Sketches and ideas came together in Figma, forming an early concept.

Further iterative wireframing in Figma highlighted challenges like meeting diverse user needs within limited screen space, balancing information density, and maintaining alignment with Tweakers' design system.

When the design was ready and all possible interactions were accounted for, I leveraged my technical skills to make a prototype using code, providing a comprehensive non-linear testing environment.

Validation

Usability tests helped gather initial feedback from participants, as well as discover the low-hanging fruit regarding usability and discoverability.

The iteration that followed performed significantly better in the follow-up test, and the completed prototype was received positively on the community forum.

Solution

View the prototype

As a drawer on the Pricewatch page, the tool provides insights into price-performance ratios, component information, ensures compatibility, and offers recommendations from the Tweakers editorial team.

Key Features

  • Provides valuable insights into what users care most about when choosing components
  • Safeguards users from potential mistakes by providing both feed forward and feedback
  • Integration with Tweakers' Pricewatch, leveraging its extensive filters and price tracking across popular Dutch web shops
  • Fill the tool with editorial recommendations at the click of a button, offering a good starting point for users or options to consider
  • User-friendly and described as clear and easy to use during usability testing
  • Responsively designed and validated for both desktop and mobile use

Potential Impact

By making it easier for users to plan their custom PC builds on the Tweakers website, I anticipate that users will make more use of referal links as they explore their options or finalize their purchases at web shops, and engage more with ad-sponsored editorial content as they seek to further inform their purchase decisions.
