PC Builder Tool

Graduation Project Tweakers UX Designer Spring 2023

Tweakers.net is a Dutch consumer electronics and technology-focused website well known for its community, editorial content, and the Pricewatch, a comprehensive price comparison tool and important driver of website referals.

The project stemmed from the premise that Tweakers users, known for their enthusiasm in building PCs, would find value in a tool designed to assist them.

My Role

As part of my graduation project and internship, I independently managed the end-to-end design process for a conceptual PC Builder tool. My responsibilities included:

  • User research
  • Market research
  • Stakeholder management
  • Requirement definition
  • Brainstorming
  • Wireframing
  • Prototyping
  • User validation

Process

Research Insights

User and market research (interviews, surveys, competitive analysis) highlighted the need for a PC building tool and revealed key user motivations, goals, and obstacles. It also identified opportunities for Tweakers to leverage its extensive database and expertise in computer parts.

  • Users are well-seasoned in building PCs, but despite their experience they still make mistakes, picking incompatible components
  • Price-performance is, by far, the most important metric to users when choosing components
  • Users inform their decisions through recommendations, ranging from personal connections to YouTube personalities
  • Competitors often feature their tool on a separate page, resulting in a disjointed user experience of navigating back and forth between pages.
  • Competitors lack focus on the Dutch market, providing incomplete or inaccurate information on component availability and pricing

Ideation

At first I employed sketching and brainstorming techniques like Crazy Eight to synthesize rough but tangible ideas to meet product requirements.

Wireframing the early concept direction in FigJam to pitch for further feedback from stakeholders.

Further iterative and higher fidelity design in Figma highlighted challenges, such as meeting the requirements within limited screen space, balancing information density, and maintaining alignment with Tweakers' design system.

A prototype was made using code, providing a comprehensive non-linear testing environment.

Validation

Initial Findings

Usability tests helped gather initial feedback from participants, as well as discover the low-hanging fruit regarding usability and discoverability.

  • The tool was collapsed by default, significantly hurting its discoverability; participants would not even open the tool unprompted
  • The tool didn't prevent user error, but would only correct the users afterwards
  • The tool also didn't provide the participants with a next step after an error was made

Outcome

The final iteration addressed the identified problems, with the following improvements evident during later testing:

  • With the tool expanded by default, participants immediately discovered the tool and started using it
  • Partipants approached their scenario of building a PC much more smoothly thanks to the feed forward highlighting the correct choices.
  • Participants were much more positive about the tool in general, they found the user experience smooth and easy to use
  • The particpants left me with a few more additions to consider, such as being able to share the list with family or friends.

Solution

  • Provides valuable insights into what users care most about when choosing components
  • Safeguards users from potential mistakes by providing both feed forward and feedback
  • Fill the tool with editorial recommendations at the click of a button
  • One page solution enabling a seamless user experience when browsing and picking components
  • Leverages Pricewatch integration to accurately provide users with the lowest available prices and availability in the Netherlands

Outcome

  • Facilitated easier planning of custom PC builds on the Tweakers website, offering a tailored experience to Dutch and Flemish PC builders compared to foreign competitors
  • Anticipated increased usage of referral links during exploration and purchase finalization
  • Expected higher engagement with ad-sponsored editorial content for informed purchase decisions

Final Thoughts

This was an invaluable experience as a real-world end-to-end design project. While conceptual as a graduation project, I remained focused on real-world potential and practicality, for instance making sure to investigate the technical feasability as well.

If time had permitted it I would've liked to dive deeper into and learn more about justifying the viability of the concept to stakeholders, especially from a business standpoint. I also would've liked to expand the scope of the tool and explore more user journeys, for example envisioning of what comes after a user saves their component list.
